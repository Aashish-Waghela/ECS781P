Threat Notification

Thousands of Israeli websites defaced due to a Cyberattack on hosting provider 'uPress' via vulnerable WordPress plugin

Incident

Type: Incident | Issue Date : 22-05-2020 | TLP: Green | Industry: All | Region: All | Serial: G-TN-EN-01-13012

Summary

On May 21, 2020, according to news media articles, thousands of Israeli websites were affected in a Cyberattack, where hackers posted anti-Israeli video and messages on compromised websites. The compromised websites also requested permission from visitors to access their webcams. The attack occurred in the wake of Israel celebrating Jerusalem Day. Though victims claim that Iranian threat actors conducted the attacks, there is no evidence suggesting these claims. The cyberattacks were operated by a threat group calling themselves "Hackers_Of_Savior", which allegedly consists of nine members from countries such as Turkey, Palestine, Morocco, and Egypt.

 

The hacks occurred due to a supply chain attack on hosting provider 'uPress'. At least 2000 websites are believed to be compromised due to the attack, with most of them being hosted on 'uPress', an Israeli WordPress webpage hosting service. uPress admitted that a vulnerability in a WordPress plugin enabled the hacks. According to security researcher Ran Bar-Zik, the threat actors edited the 'wp-includes/version.php' wordpress file with plugins that were deployed across all compromised websites, to include the Anti-Israel content. The compromised websites included a script that requested access to users' webcams and tried to take a photo of the user and upload it to a remote server. The threat actors also included a plugin that attempted to delete database tables, though it is unclear if they succeeded in those attempts.

 

The hacked websites included websites of local municipalities, religious and cultural organizations, popular restaurant chains, several NGOs and educational institutes. uPress took down most of the compromised websites and pulled the file hackers were exploiting. However cached versions of the websites may still be available online.

 

It is possible that such campaigns could have second-hand effects for U.S. organizations with ties to Israel. Hacktivist activity occasionally escalates in conjunction with certain significant dates, as Deloitte CTI previously reported during the "OpJeruslam" and "OpIsrael," Anonymous-affiliated 2018-2020 campaigns. For example, on March 2, 2019, attackers using the hashtag "OpJerusalem" targeted the hosting provider of Nagich, a popular Israeli third-party accessibility JavaScript plugin, which was implemented by hundreds of Israeli websites. As a result, effected websites visitors were presented with a defacement message and were prompted to download a Jcry ransomware variant masquerading as an Adobe Flash update.

Recommendations

Deloitte CTI recommends users to update their Wordpress plugins to the latest versions from the official websites to avoid any loophole being left for an attacker to exploit.
